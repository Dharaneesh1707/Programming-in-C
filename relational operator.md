Relational operators (or comparison operators) are ==symbols in programming used to compare two values (operands) and determine the relationship between them, resulting in a boolean output (true/false or 1/0)==. They are fundamental for decision-making and controlling program flow, used in `if` statements and loops, and include common symbols like `==` (equal to), `!=` (not equal to), `>` (greater than), `<` (less than), `>=` (greater than or equal to), and `<=` (less than or equal to). 

This video provides a hands-on introduction to relational operators in C and C++:
Common Relational Operators

- **`==`**: Equal to (e.g., `x == 5`)

- **`!=`**: Not equal to (e.g., `x != 5`)

- **`>`**: Greater than (e.g., `x > 5`)

- **`<`**: Less than (e.g., `x < 5`)

- **`>=`**: Greater than or equal to (e.g., `x >= 5`)

- **`<=`**: Less than or equal to (e.g., `x <= 5`)
